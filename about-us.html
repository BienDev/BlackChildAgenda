THE BLACK CHILD AGENDA ”Today’s strategy for better tomorrow”
The Black Child Agenda exists to improve the future prospects of Black children in the UK. We do this by working directly with children, parents, families the wider community, organisations and institutions to address issues within the Black community affecting our children such as:

  • Family breakdowns
  • Culturally inappropriate education
  • Poor economic development
  • School exclusions
  • Gang culture

These issues prevent the Black community from progressing and those most affected are the most vulnerable amongst us - our children! See what we do to combat disadvantage in our community.

The Black Child Agenda was founded in 2013 by Cheryl Pheonix after she found her son being continually being place in isolation at school (any more info about what happened here?). Having experienced first hand the difficulties of being a black parent supporting a black child through the system, Cheryl now extends support to other families facing similar barriers and inequality.

Since then other members have joined the team, and all bring a wealth of knowledge and experience in their own area of expertise. The TBCA team have a unique mix of skills and understanding in the key areas of Family rights, education and home schooling, the criminal justice system and social services – a meeting of minds with a passion for progression!

We understand the importance of keeping families together, equipping parents to successfully raise and nurture their children to be the best that they can be. We help with:
  • Fathers and Families by providing support to fathers on visitation and access rights, court cases and maintaining a healthy relationship with child, mother and wider family
  • Parental support including giving advice on safeguarding and social services intervention, advice on parenting techniques and a parental support network.
